# Self & Developer Critique

## Self-Critique

### What did we do well?
We were able to compile a very large number of resources, organizations, and events from various sources in a format that our users could navigate easily. We were also able to implement helpful features like filtering / sorting by location.

---

### What did we learn?
We learned how to programmatically scrape data, load it into a database, and query it using an API we designed. We learned how to dynamically render our content and query subsets of our data to return as results of filtering/sorting and searching. In terms of soft skills, we learned how to work on a project with multiple moving parts. Communicating with each other, asking for help when needed and ensuring the process moves smoothly was very important.

---

### What did we teach each other?
We taught each other how to navigate challenges and work through technical difficulties. We also learned new skills from group members who had previously worked with some parts of the tech stack.

---

### What can we do better?
We can implement extra features such as an interactive map, and flesh out our events model more. We could also separate our instances into more specific models like medical aid, legal services, etc.

---

### What effect did the peer reviews have?
Having an outsider perspective on our project was really helpful. Hearing what a potential user would actually like to use gave us good insight in what to implement. Reviewing our developer’s website helped us anticipate possible problems in our own project.

---

### What puzzles us?
We are puzzled by our struggle to get API keys from the 211 API portal, and the difficulty we had in scraping events. We were able to circumvent these issues, but it was frustrating to have our progress hindered by factors outside our control.

---

## Developer’s Critique

### What did they do well?
Their website is well-organized and the navigation is smooth. The implemented features are helpful and demonstrate that the group understands how to leverage APIs well! The experience of using their website is informative and intuitive.

---

### How effective was their RESTful API?
Their RESTful API was very effective. The results returned as the result of search queries and the filtering and sorting is done with very little latency and is very professional.

---

### How well did they implement your user stories?
They implemented our user stories very well! They delivered what we asked for, and if there was ever a discrepancy in the requirements or the story or execution need to be modified, they communicated effectively and in a timely manner with us.

---

### What did we learn from their website?
We learned how to effectively use the Google Maps API, and additional features we could include that would be helpful to our users. The data sources they used would also be helpful if we wanted to expand our database.

---

### What can they do better?
They could add more instances to cover a larger area. Right now, it seems like most of the instances are from the Austin area, so it would be nice if they could expand to cover other metropolitan areas in Texas and eventually in more states.

---

### What puzzles us about their website?
The total count that displays on the model pages is confusing. We originally thought that the count displayed was the total number of instances they had of the model, but it seems that it refers to the number of instances being shown on the page. It would be great if this could be more clear, by labeling it like “Showing: 20 Results” or something similar.
